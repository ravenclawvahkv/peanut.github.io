FAQs

1 Is peanut butter good for diabetics?? +
Peanuts and peanut butter help to reduce spikes in blood sugar, and may improve long-term glucose control throughout the day. Peanuts also help keep you fuller, longer.
2 Do peanuts increase cholesterol? +
No. Peanuts contain 0 cholesterol, and can actually lower high levels of LDL, or “bad,” cholesterol. High LDL cholesterol can lead to atherosclerosis—fatty deposits that build up inside your arteries.Fortunately, different components of peanuts include healthy oils, protein, and fiber, which can all help reduce cholesterol.
3 Are peanuts good for high blood pressure? +
Yes, studies show that eating peanuts regularly helps decrease blood pressure, even among individuals with high blood pressure: One study found that “participants with elevated blood pressure at baseline had significant decreases in diastolic blood pressure” after peanut consumption.
4 Can peanut sorting machines handle different peanut varieties? +
Yes, modern peanut sorting machines are designed to handle various peanut varieties effectively. They can be adjusted to accommodate different sizes, shapes, and colors of peanuts
5 What is peanut sorting? +
Peanut sorting is the process of separating peanuts based on various attributes such as size, color, shape, and defects. It ensures that only high-quality peanuts meeting specific standards are packed for consumption or further processing.
.
6 Does peanut sorting affect the nutritional value of peanuts? +
No, peanut sorting does not significantly affect the nutritional value of peanuts. It primarily focuses on removing physical defects and contaminants while preserving the inherent nutritional content of the peanuts.
7 Are there regulations governing peanut sorting? +
Depending on the region, there may be regulations or industry standards governing peanut sorting practices to ensure food safety and quality standards are met.
8 What are the nutritional benefits of peanuts? +
Peanuts are a good source of protein, fiber, healthy fats
9 Are peanuts nuts or legumes? +
Despite the name, peanuts are technically legumes, belonging to the same family as beans and lentils. However, they share similar nutritional characteristics with tree nuts and are often treated as such in culinary contexts and allergy considerations
10 How common is peanut allergy? +
Peanut allergy is one of the most common food allergies, especially among children. Its prevalence has been increasing in recent years, affecting approximately 1-2% of the population
